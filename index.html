The magnitude and distribution Japanese earthquake (2000-2022)

The data, collected from the USGS website, records historical earthquake data of magnitude 6 or greater that have occurred in Japan since 2000. The data set contains information on the time, location, intensity and magnitude of each major earthquake, as well as the focal depth.

Background About Japan

Japan's is located at the intersection of multiple tectonic plates, coupled with related trenches and active volcanic activity, makes it an earthquake-prone region.

About the earthquake

Magnitude: a scale that quantifies the energy release of an earthquake. It is a logarithmic scale, where an increase of 1.0 indicates roughly 32 times more energy release.

Focal Depth: describes the depth underground where the earthquake rupture begins.

Scale: hinged on the magnitude of the earthquake
Moderate(5.5 - 6.0), Strong(6.1 - 6.9), Major(7.0 - 7.9), Great(8.0 or greater).
